# ClaudeFluent Audience Personas

## Persona 1: Ashley — Director of Product Marketing, Series B SaaS

- 38, Director of PMM at a 200-person B2B SaaS. TC ~$180k. Manages a team of 3 PMMs.
- Owns the full GTM motion. Accountable to the CMO.
- Feels behind personally AND worried her team looks behind. Sees competitors cranking out content faster and suspects AI is why.
- Has dabbled with ChatGPT but never gone deep. Doesn't know what Claude Code is.
- Dual motivation: (1) personally get faster, (2) figure out how to roll this out to her team as a force multiplier.
- Budget: would expense $500-800 without blinking. Would push for a team license if good.

**What resonates in content:**
- Team-level framing: "Here's how a marketing team uses this"
- Specific marketing workflow examples (competitive analysis, launch briefs, sales enablement)
- Director-level testimonials and ROI stories
- Evidence it works for non-engineers

**Language that converts:**
- "Your team can ship this in a day instead of a sprint"
- "No engineering background required"
- "Here's what a PMM built in their first session"

**Language that bounces:**
- Developer-heavy jargon without translation
- Individual-only framing (she's thinking about her team)
- No org-level ROI story

## Persona 2: Derek — Solo Entrepreneur

- 36, left corporate PM job 18 months ago. Building a niche SaaS tool. Has runway.
- Follows every AI newsletter, has tried Cursor, Replit, v0. Knows enough to be dangerous but can't ship fast enough.
- Sees AI as the lever that lets him compete with funded teams.
- Very online. Will Google the instructor, check Twitter/X, look for free alternatives. Due diligence, not frugality.
- High BS tolerance. Will call out vague promises fast.

**What resonates in content:**
- Proof the instructor actually builds things (shipping velocity, real products)
- Specific technical outcomes with timelines ("built X in 3 hours")
- Community of other ambitious builders
- Practitioner credibility, not corporate polish

**Language that converts:**
- "I built this in a weekend with Claude Code" (with proof)
- "Here's the exact prompt I used"
- "This is the unfair advantage serious founders are using"

**Language that bounces:**
- Vague promises without evidence
- Corporate-speak or marketing fluff
- Theoretical content without real examples
- No evidence of instructor's own building

## Persona 3: Priya — Senior Product Manager, Mid-size Tech

- 33, Senior PM at a 400-person company. Manages 2 squads. TC ~$160k.
- Uses Claude.ai daily for writing and research but has never touched the CLI.
- Frustrated that she has to wait on engineers for prototypes, data pulls, automations.
- Time-starved. If the value isn't clear in 30 seconds, she's gone.
- Primary goal: reduce dependency on eng, prototype faster, impress stakeholders with output velocity.

**What resonates in content:**
- PM-specific workflows and use cases
- Time-to-value framing ("built a working prototype before standup")
- Evidence that non-engineers succeed
- Self-paced, respect for her time constraints

**Language that converts:**
- "Stop waiting on engineering for this"
- "Here's what a PM built in week 1"
- "15 minutes to set up, then you're building"

**Language that bounces:**
- Feels like it requires becoming a developer
- No time commitment stated
- No PM-specific use cases

## Persona 4: Sandra — VP of Customer Success, Series C SaaS

- 44, VP of CS. Manages 18 CSMs and 2 team leads. Reports to CRO.
- Not technical. Her edge is process design, retention strategy, executive relationships.
- CEO pushing "AI efficiency" as a board-level initiative. Asked to present a plan for how CS will use AI to improve NRR without adding headcount.
- Thinking both personally (how do I use this?) and organizationally (how do I roll this out?).
- Decision is slower and more deliberate. Wants ROI framing, not just features.

**What resonates in content:**
- CS-specific workflow examples (churn analysis, QBR prep, health score summaries)
- ROI story she can take to her CRO
- Team/seat pricing and rollout planning
- Business case framing

**Language that converts:**
- "Here's the ROI calculation for your board deck"
- "Your CSMs can automate these 3 tasks this week"
- "No technical background needed"

**Language that bounces:**
- Only engineer or solo operator framing
- No team plan or org-level thinking
- No business case framing

## How to Frame the Same Topic for Each Persona

**Example topic: "Claude Code can build internal tools"**

- **Ashley:** "Your marketing team can build their own dashboards instead of waiting 6 weeks for engineering."
- **Derek:** "I built a customer feedback analyzer in 2 hours that would've taken a contractor $3k and 2 weeks."
- **Priya:** "Pull your own data, build your own prototypes. Stop opening Jira tickets for things you could do yourself."
- **Sandra:** "Your CSMs can build health score automations that run every morning. Here's the ROI math."
