## ClaudeFluent Default Interpretation

For ClaudeFluent, assume the business is premium professional upskilling for operators and nontechnical knowledge workers unless the user says otherwise.

Recommended source weighting:

- 35% cohort/expert course mechanics from Maven, Wes Kao, Gagan Biyani, and altMBA.
- 25% offer, pricing, and acquisition from Hormozi and Acquisition.com.
- 20% professional upskilling from Reforge, Section, and CXL.
- 10% coding bootcamp rigor from Codesmith, Flatiron, and App Academy.
- 10% instructional design from Julie Dirksen, Make It Stick, and Learning How to Learn.

Avoid bootcamp-style job-placement positioning unless the offer actually includes placement infrastructure, audited outcomes, and legal review.

## What to Extract From Sources

When refreshing content, capture:

- Exact promise and named transformation.
- Target student/customer and prerequisite level.
- Price, duration, format, cadence, cohort size, and support model.
- Proof: testimonials, case studies, outcomes reports, artifacts, reviews, screenshots, placement data.
- Curriculum structure: sequence, projects, practice, feedback, accountability, assessment.
- Funnel: free content, workshops, webinars, sales calls, application, community, referrals, affiliate motion.
- Risk reversal: guarantee, refund policy, deferred tuition, ISA, outcome-based pricing, or no guarantee.
- Community mechanics: onboarding, rituals, leaderboards, peer feedback, alumni loops, ongoing value.
- Margin risks: instructor time, TA/support load, refund rate, cohort fill risk, platform dependency.

## Red Flags

- Vague outcome: "learn X" instead of "use X to achieve Y."
- Curriculum as content library with no practice loop.
- Testimonials about vibes but no applied wins.
- Income or job claims without methodology.
- Financing that hides true cost or shifts risk onto students.
- Low-ticket front end that attracts students who cannot succeed in the premium back end.
- Community positioned as value but mostly functioning as a chat room.
- AI content generation used to increase volume instead of applied practice, feedback, and personalization.
