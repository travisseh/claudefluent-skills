# ClaudeFluent Personas

| Persona | Role | Company Stage | Primary Goal |
|---------|------|--------------|--------------|
| Ashley | Director of Product Marketing | Series B SaaS | Personal + team AI upskill |
| Derek | Solo Entrepreneur | Self-funded | Ship faster, build something big |
| Priya | Senior Product Manager | Mid-size tech | Reduce eng dependency |
| Sandra | VP of Customer Success | Series C SaaS | Team AI rollout for NRR |

---

## Persona 1: Ashley — Director of Product Marketing, Series B SaaS

- 38, Director of PMM at a 200-person B2B SaaS (HR tech or fintech). TC ~$180k. Manages a team of 3 PMMs.
- Owns the full GTM motion — positioning, launches, competitive, sales enablement. Accountable to the CMO.
- Feels behind personally AND worried her team looks behind. Sees competitors' marketing orgs cranking out content and campaigns faster and suspects AI is why.
- Has dabbled with ChatGPT but never gone deep. Her engineers keep mentioning Claude Code and she doesn't know what it is.
- Dual motivation: (1) personally get faster and smarter, (2) figure out how to roll this out to her team as a force multiplier — this is a leadership play, not just a personal upskill.
- Budget: would expense $500–800 without blinking. Would push for a team license if it's good.

**Key Questions:**
- "Is there a team plan or bulk pricing?"
- "What workflows can I hand off to my PMMs?"
- "How do I roll this out without it becoming shelfware?"
- "Is this actually for non-engineers or will my team bounce after day one?"

**Red Flags:** Only individual-focused, no team use cases, no org-level ROI story, developer-heavy language

**What Converts Her:** "Here's how a marketing team uses this" framing, team/seat pricing option, director-level testimonial, specific marketing workflow examples

## Persona 2: Derek — Solo Entrepreneur

- 36, left his corporate PM job 18 months ago. Building a niche SaaS tool. Has some runway and isn't sweating it.
- Follows every AI newsletter, has tried Cursor, Replit, v0. Knows enough to be dangerous but can't ship fast enough.
- Deeply motivated by the vision — wants to build something that matters, something that scales. Thinks about it constantly.
- Sees AI as the lever that lets him compete with funded teams. If Claude Code means he can ship in days what used to take weeks, that's not a cost — that's the whole game.
- Very online. Will Google the instructor, check Twitter/X, look for free alternatives — not because he's cheap but because he vets everything. Due diligence, not frugality.
- High BS tolerance — he'll dig into the details and call out vague promises fast.

**Key Questions:**
- "Who's the instructor and are they actually building things themselves?"
- "What's the fastest path from zero to shipping?"
- "Is there a community of other builders?"
- "Can I see a sample lesson?"

**Red Flags:** No instructor credibility signals, no free content to sample, feels theoretical rather than practitioner-taught, no evidence of real products built

**What Converts Him:** Proof of instructor's own shipping velocity, specific technical outcomes, a community of ambitious builders, the sense that this is the unfair advantage serious founders are using

## Persona 3: Priya — Senior Product Manager, Mid-size Tech

- 33, Senior PM at a 400-person company. Manages 2 squads. TC ~$160k.
- Uses Claude.ai daily for writing and research but has never touched the CLI or API.
- Frustrated that she has to wait on engineers for things she feels she could do herself — prototypes, data pulls, automations.
- Time-starved. Evaluates purchases fast. If the homepage doesn't explain the value in 30 seconds she's gone.
- Primary goal: reduce dependency on eng for small tasks, prototype ideas faster, impress stakeholders with output velocity.
- Will share with her team if it's good — potential multiplier.

**Key Questions:**
- "How much time per week does this require?"
- "Is this self-paced?"
- "What does someone like me actually build by the end?"

**Red Flags:** No time commitment stated, feels like it requires becoming a developer, no PM-specific use cases

**What Converts Her:** "Here's what a PM built in week 1" testimonial, self-paced format, specific PM workflow examples

---

## Persona 4: Sandra — VP of Customer Success, Series C SaaS

- 44, VP of CS at a 300-person Series C. Manages a team of 18 CSMs and 2 team leads. Reports to CRO.
- Not technical — her edge is process design, retention strategy, and executive relationships.
- CEO is pushing "AI efficiency" as a board-level initiative. She's been asked to present a plan for how CS will use AI to improve NRR without adding headcount. She's mildly panicked.
- Thinking both personally (how do I use this?) and organizationally (how do I build a case and roll this out to 18 people?).
- Decision is slower and more deliberate. Will want to see ROI framing, not just features.

**Key Questions:**
- "Can I actually build something useful without being technical?"
- "Do you have CS-specific examples?"
- "Is there team pricing? Can I expense this for my whole team?"
- "What's the ROI story I can take to my CRO?"

**Red Flags:** No CS use cases, no team plan, no business case framing, feels like it's only for engineers or solo operators

**What Converts Her:** A CS-specific workflow example (churn analysis, QBR prep, health score summaries), team/seat pricing, an ROI framing she can reuse internally
